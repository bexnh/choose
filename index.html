Cat's Howl

You step out into the Front yard of an old Victorian home with your Father. Your Mother and Brother are gone already. The lights are on in the living room, and you can see the flickering of a TV.

Back to your right there is a picket fence with a half-open Gate. Your Father strays towards the Front porch. What do you do?

You step up onto the porch to knock on the Front door, and as you do it creaks open. The Living Room is to your right, with its flickering TV and an unlit fireplace. You find your Mother there, and she comes to stay by your side. You won't see your brother for a while.

Straight ahead you see an empty Kitchen, and there's a particularly loud hum coming from the fridge. You turn to your Mother and Father. What do you do?

You open the Gate all the way and step through, as your Father enters the house. On the other side of the fence, a flowering vine grows from beside the gate along a short path towards the Back.

As you peer along this side of the house, a lamp switches on in a room on the Second Floor. You wonder where your various relatives may be, relative to where you are. What do you do?

The three of you move into the warmly lit Living Room. Your Mother moves past the Fireplace into the Dining Room, your Father slowly fallowing her across the room.

The Fireplace is empty and cold, almost drafty. Not almost; it’s open to a short dark tunnel you could just duck inside. What do you do?

You move forward, noticing in passing a set of stairs to your left. The Kitchen is brightly lit, with soft white light emanating from the Fridge.

To you right you see the Dining Room, lights on but the wooden table unset, and your Mother and Father have already made their way inside. What do you do?

Passing by the light above, you move Back along the side of the house towards a colorful Garden, almost luminescent in the night.

You glance back towards where you Started, towards your Mother and Father­­. What do you do?

You retreat until you can enter the house, blowing past other paths and possibilities with your excitement to get up the stairs. Here on the Second Floor, the lamp doesn’t hold your interest for long. To your right is a Bathroom, the black and white tiles making you dizzy for a long moment.

Opposite is a Bedroom, where you are certain you will find a plush bed covered in soft blankets. What do you do?

You peek inside the Fireplace and know you could Crawl though and exit somewhere outside, beyond, far from this home and your family.

They are behind you, already exploring the empty Dining Room. What do you do?

This Dining Room is empty save for you and your kin. Not even a trace of dinner or dinnerware mars the table. To your left is the cool glow of the Kitchen.

The thought makes you hungry, and you consider taking this chance to Dine. What do you do?

You pull the handle on the Fridge door, pulling against the resistance of the sealed edges. Save for the shallow shelves of the door itself, it is empty. Bereft. Void.

The light inside threatens to overtake you, pulling you In. Your Hunger threatens as well, and the food in the door will happily provide. What do you do?

The Garden is indeed glowing, the life itself beaming into the night. You follow the cleared path through this luminous forest.

The thin spires of light tempt you to Sit and stare till dawn, just as you hear a sharp crack in the Distance. What do you do?

You are rewarded with the coziest sight you’ve seen. It occurs to you that you are more tired than you should be, at this time of night—What time is it?—and for how long you’ve been in the house—How long again?

This Bed beckons, but there is a Bookshelf littered with jutting spines as well. What do you do?

Here in the Bathroom you lose your footing from the dizzying wobbly tiling. You are tempted to close the door for some Privacy.

On the back wall there is a place for laundry, including a Dryer with its door ajar. What do you do?

You have to get on hands and knees to Crawl through this space built for embers. When you rise, dusting off your hands and knees, you are in another world.

This cave is a cool blue yet it glows a warm pink. There are tunnels that may go on forever. Down one you see a Ladder; not wrought from stone but metal bolted to the wall. Down another there are shafts of light piercing down from natural ceiling. Not light but substance, hard and brilliant. You think they could be Bones, the ribs of something enormous. What do you do?

You lean ever further into the emptiness, tripping over the bottom edge as you tumble into the appliance. Blinded for just a second, your eyes adjust to the harsh white passage. At its end there is a palm print, stark black on the wall.

You glance back towards the Kitchen, even as your Hand reaches forwards. What do you do?

You read the titles on the Bookshelf, seeing familiar and stranger alike. Your favorite jumps out at you, and you grab it without another thought.

As you pull it from it’s place, the Bookshelf pulls away from the Wall with it. This is intriguing, but you’ve never resisted the pull of a Story before. What do you do?

You pry open the Dryer all the way. You can hardly fathom its interior, lined not with metal or linen, but with blue light seemingly stitched into night itself.

You are frightened, and remember your Mother and Father in the Living Room downstairs. You are excited, and ponder the possibilities that lie Within. What do you do?
